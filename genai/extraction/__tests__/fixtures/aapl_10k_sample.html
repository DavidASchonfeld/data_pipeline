Part I

Item 1. Business

The Company designs, manufactures and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables and accessories, and sells a variety of related services. The Company's fiscal year is the 52- or 53-week period that ends on the last Saturday of September. Products include flagship devices and a growing services segment spanning advertising, cloud, payments and subscriptions.

Item 1A. Risk Factors

The Company's business, results of operations and financial condition are subject to numerous risks and uncertainties. Global and regional economic conditions could materially adversely affect demand. The Company faces substantial competition and rapid technological change. Supply chain concentration, component shortages and reliance on outsourcing partners create operational risk. Legal and regulatory developments across many jurisdictions may increase costs or limit operations.

Part II

Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

This section discusses the Company's results of operations and liquidity. Net sales increased year over year, driven by services growth and stronger product mix, partially offset by foreign exchange headwinds. Gross margin expanded due to favorable cost structure and a higher services mix. The Company returned capital to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases while maintaining a strong balance sheet and significant cash and marketable securities.

Item 7A. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk

The Company is exposed to market risk from changes in interest rates, foreign currency exchange rates and equity prices. Interest rate risk arises from the investment portfolio and outstanding debt; the Company uses derivative instruments to hedge a portion of these exposures. Foreign currency risk arises from sales and costs denominated in currencies other than the U.S. dollar.
